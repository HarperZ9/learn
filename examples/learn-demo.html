Project Telos / accountable credential and coursework engine

learn

Study smarter, prove it honestly, and never let the machine take the test for you.

Course platforms automate the logistics but never tell you whether you actually learned anything, and a generic AI tutor will hand you the answer if you ask twice. learn automates the logistics, runs a real learning loop against your own practice, and halts hard at every step that is supposed to prove you know it.

Accountability spine

witness

every step is content-addressed (sha256 digest of exactly what happened)

ledger

hash-chained, tamper-evident log of the run

gate

default-deny; an undeclared step kind is refused, nothing actuated

receipt

separates automated logistics from the operator's own graded work

A run of examples/course.json

Every step actuates against the offline FakeDriver, in order, until it reaches the graded step. There it halts. The engine does not open the quiz, does not guess an answer, and does not advance past it on its own.

  1. navigate
  2. waitFor
  3. click
  4. capture
  5. assess — HALT (Quiz 1)
  6. complete

The operator does the quiz. Only then does resume with an attestation carry the run to complete, and the receipt keeps the split: what the engine automated, and what the operator did themselves.

The learning loop, over examples/study.json

plan

objectives, some with prerequisites (a concept map, not a flat list)

due

spaced-repetition scheduler flags what needs review, most-overdue first

practice

the operator answers; only their own attempts are ever recorded

misconceptions

wrong attempts and feedback are aggregated, ranked by count

mastery-gate

ready only once every objective clears threshold accuracy over enough attempts

readiness

an objective unlocks only once every objective it requires is itself mastered

mastery() is a function of the operator's own scored practice attempts only. It never reads a render, a visualization, or a pending prediction as if it were a graded answer.
